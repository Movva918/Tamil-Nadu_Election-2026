Q8 — Postal Votes % vs Voter Turnout % Correlation
Pearson correlation between postal vote % and overall constituency turnout %.
+0.171
Pearson r — 2021
Weak positive. Rural/service constituencies with more postal votes had slightly higher turnout — not a strong signal.
−0.016
Pearson r — 2026
Near-zero. The massive uniform turnout surge erased any prior relationship between postal votes and turnout.
Interpretation: Postal votes are 0.3–2.3% of totals. High turnout in 2026 was broadly distributed — not concentrated in high-postal-vote areas.

Q9 — District Literacy % vs Voter Turnout % Correlation
Census 2011 district literacy rates vs average constituency turnout per district.
−0.720
Pearson r — Literacy vs 2021 Turnout
Strong negative. Higher-literacy districts (Chennai, Coimbatore) had significantly lower turnout in 2021 — urban apathy was real.
−0.457
Pearson r — Literacy vs 2026 Turnout
Moderate negative — weakened as urban constituencies surged. Chennai went from 59% to 84% avg turnout, closing the gap.
Urbanisation paradox: In 2021 the most literate districts voted least (r=−0.72). In 2026 urban areas saw the biggest surges (+26–30pp in Chennai Metro), weakening the correlation to −0.46.
